Cons of staying physical-only or website-only (what you lose)

We’ll be direct: these are the main business costs of not having an app.

Physical Shop — Limitations & Costs

  • Limited reach: Your customers are mostly local. You miss buyers from other areas.
  • Fixed hours: Sales stop when the shop closes. An app sells 24/7.
  • Manual inventory: Counting and updating stock takes time and leads to errors.
  • Staff dependency: You need staff to manage orders and customer calls — this increases payroll and mistakes.
  • Higher local costs: Rent, utilities, and maintenance do not directly scale with sales.

Website Only — Hidden Weaknesses

  • Re-engagement is expensive: You rely on ads and emails to bring customers back — these cost money every month.
  • Slow conversion: Mobile websites have more steps; drop-offs are common at checkout.
  • No instant reach: You can’t send real-time offers that appear directly as notifications on a phone.
  • Maintenance: Website hosting, updates, and plugin costs add up month after month.
  • Marketplace fees: If you use marketplaces, commissions cut into profits.

Costs & Maintenance — clear view (no surprises)

You asked for honesty. Here’s what to expect compared to current business costs.

Physical store ongoing costs (typical)

  • Rent & utilities (fixed monthly)
  • Staff wages and training
  • Inventory holding costs
  • Local marketing (flyers, local ads)

Website ongoing costs (typical)

  • Hosting and domain fees
  • Plugin and platform updates
  • Ad spend for traffic (Google, Facebook)
  • Developer or support hours for fixes

App ongoing costs (what you should expect)

  • Maintenance: Regular updates and minor changes — smaller than multiple website fixes because codebase is unified.
  • Hosting & backend: Firebase hosting and database costs scale with usage; typically low for small-to-medium stores.
  • Payment gateway fees: Same as website — transaction fee per sale.
  • Optional marketing: You can use push notifications (low-cost) instead of high ad spend.

Overall, adding an app can reduce your ongoing marketing spend while increasing conversion and repeat sales. The initial setup is one time; maintenance is predictable and much smaller than many ongoing costs of running a physical store or expensive ad campaigns.
